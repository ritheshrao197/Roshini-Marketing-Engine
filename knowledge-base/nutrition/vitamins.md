# Nutrition Directory: Vitamins

Vitamins are essential micronutrients that the body needs in small amounts to carry out vital cellular functions. Roshini's Home Products are rich in natural, plant-based vitamins, avoiding synthetic fortification.

---

## 1. Vitamin E (Tocopherol)
- **Primary Source:** Mamra and Gurbandi almonds, walnuts, and pumpkin seeds.
- **Key Functions:**
  - **Antioxidant Action:** Protects cell membranes from oxidative damage.
  - **Skin & Hair Health:** Promotes cellular repair, leading to healthy skin and strong hair roots.
  - **Cognitive Protection:** Supports neurological structures and memory development.

## 2. B-Complex Vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B6, B9, B12)
- **Primary Source:** Sprouted grains (Ragi, Jowar, Bajra, Foxtail, and Little millets), peanuts, and seeds.
- **The Sprouting Effect:** Germination triggers metabolic activity inside the seed, multiplying the levels of B-vitamins—especially Thiamine (B1), Riboflavin (B2), Niacin (B3), and Folic Acid (B9)—by up to 2x to 3x.
- **Key Functions:**
  - **Energy Metabolism:** Converts carbohydrates, proteins, and fats into ATP (active body energy).
  - **Red Blood Cells:** B9 (Folate) is critical for DNA synthesis and red blood cell production, making it vital for pregnant women.
  - **Nerve Function:** Supports cognitive signaling and myelin sheath maintenance.

## 3. Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
- **Primary Source:** Sprouted millets. (While raw millets contain almost no Vitamin C, the 12-to-24-hour sprouting phase synthesizes fresh, bioavailable Vitamin C).
- **Key Functions:**
  - **Immunity:** Supports white blood cell production and skin barriers.
  - **Iron Absorption:** Vitamin C acts as a reducer in the gut, boosting the absorption rate of plant-based non-heme iron from sprouted Bajra and seeds.
