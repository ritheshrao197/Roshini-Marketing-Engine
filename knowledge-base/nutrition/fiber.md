# Nutrition Directory: Dietary Fiber

Dietary fiber is a type of carbohydrate that the body cannot digest. Unlike other carbs that break down into sugar molecules, fiber passes through the digestive tract intact, playing a critical role in gut health and glycemic control.

---

## 1. Types of Fiber in Our Products

Our ancient grains, premium nuts, and seeds provide a healthy balance of both fiber types:

### Soluble Fiber
- **Primary Source:** Flax seeds, Chia seeds, and Bajra.
- **How it works:** Dissolves in water to form a gel-like substance in the gut.
- **Benefits:** Slows down glucose absorption (preventing sudden blood sugar spikes) and helps maintain healthy cholesterol levels.

### Insoluble Fiber
- **Primary Source:** Finger millet (Ragi), Jowar, Barnyard millet, and nut skins.
- **How it works:** Does not dissolve in water. It adds bulk to stool.
- **Benefits:** Promotes healthy peristalsis (bowel movements), preventing constipation and keeping the digestive tract clean.

---

## 2. Key Health Benefits

### 1. Steady Energy (Low Glycemic Index)
- By slowing down the rate at which food is broken down, fiber ensures a steady, slow-release of glucose into the bloodstream. This prevents the "sugar crashes" associated with commercial refined breakfasts.

### 2. Prebiotic Gut Health
- Fiber acts as a prebiotic, serving as food for the beneficial bacteria (probiotics) residing in the gut. A healthy gut microbiome improves digestion, nutrient assimilation, and overall immunity.

### 3. Satiety (Fullness)
- Fiber-rich meals take longer to chew and expand in the stomach, promoting feelings of fullness and satiety, which supports healthy weight management.
