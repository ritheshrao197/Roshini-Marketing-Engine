# The Manufacturing Process: Traditional Small-Batch Preparation

Our manufacturing process is designed to respect traditional preparation methods while maintaining strict hygiene and safety standards. This slow, careful process ensures maximum nutritional bioavailability and freshness.

---

## 1. Step-by-Step Preparation Cycles

We operate two distinct processing tracks in our home facility to cater to different product needs:

```mermaid
graph TD
    subgraph Track A: Millet, Dry Fruits & Seeds Mixes (e.g. Nutrimix)
        A1[Sourcing & Sorting] --> A2[Washing & Cleaning]
        A2 --> A3[Sun-Drying / Dehydration]
        A3 --> A4[Slow Hand-Roasting]
    end
    subgraph Track B: Sprouted Single-Grain Flours (e.g. Sprouted Ragi)
        B1[Sourcing & Sorting] --> B2[Washing & Cleaning]
        B2 --> B3[Soaking - 12 Hours]
        B3 --> B4[Sprouting - 12 Hours]
        B4 --> B5[RO Water Rinse]
        B5 --> B6[Sun-Drying / Dehydration]
        B6 --> B7[Slow Hand-Roasting/Drying]
    end
    A4 --> C[Traditional Stone Grinding]
    B7 --> C
    C --> D[Sifting & Cooling]
    D --> E[Weighing & Sealing]
```

### Sourcing & Sorting (Both Tracks)
- All grains, nuts, and seeds are hand-sorted to remove stones, chaff, and damaged pieces. Only clean, whole grains are processed.

### Washing & Cleaning (Both Tracks)
- Grains are washed thoroughly in clean, running water 3 to 4 times to remove dirt and surface impurities.

### Track A processing (Unsprouted Mixes)
- **Sun-Drying & Dehydration:** The washed grains are spread evenly on sanitized elevated trays under direct sunlight to dry completely.
- **Slow Hand-Roasting:** The dried grains, raw nuts, and seeds are roasted separately in heavy-bottomed iron pans (kadhai) on a low-medium flame. Roasting is done slowly to develop a rich, nutty aroma, make the grains easily digestible, and lock in nutrients.

### Track B processing (Sprouted Flours)
- **Soaking:** Sorted grains are soaked in clean RO-filtered water in food-grade stainless steel containers for 12 hours.
- **Sprouting (Germination):** The soaked water is drained. Grains are tied in clean muslin cloths and suspended in a warm, dark cabinet to sprout for 12 hours. Sprouting breaks down phytic acid (an anti-nutrient) and unlocks essential minerals.
- **Rinse & Sun-Drying:** Sprouted grains are rinsed with RO water and sun-dried on sanitized elevated trays for 1 to 2 days.

### Grinding, Sifting & Packaging (Both Tracks)
- **Traditional Stone Grinding:** Ground using slow-speed stone mills. Slow grinding prevents the generation of excessive heat, preserving sensitive vitamins, healthy oils, and active enzymes.
- **Sifting, Cooling & Sealing:** Ground flour is cooled to room temperature, sifted to remove coarse husks, and immediately weighed into food-grade, moisture-proof stand-up pouches with zip locks. Each pouch is heat-sealed to preserve freshness.

## 2. Why Our Process Matters

- **Slow Hand-Roasting digestability:** Slow roasting pre-gelatinizes the starches in unsprouted millets, making them light on the stomach and easy to digest without requiring germination.
- **Targeted Sprouting (for Sprouted Flours):** The 12-hour sprouting process used for sprouted single-grain flours deactivates phytic acid and multiplies mineral absorption (calcium, iron, zinc) by nearly 300%.
- **Gluten-Free Safety:** We do not grind wheat or gluten-containing grains in our facility, completely avoiding cross-contamination.
- **No Heat Damage:** Traditional stone grinding operates at low speeds and temperatures, preserving delicate nutrients like Vitamin B, Vitamin E, and healthy fats.
- **Freshness Guarantee:** We roast and grind in small batches of 5–10 kg to fulfill recent orders, never storing stale stock on shelves.
