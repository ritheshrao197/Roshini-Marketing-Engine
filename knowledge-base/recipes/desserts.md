# Recipe Directory: Healthy Desserts & Sweet Treats

Eating healthy doesn't mean skipping dessert. These recipes provide guilt-free, nutrient-dense sweets perfect for satisfying sweet cravings naturally.

---

## 1. Kids' Healthy Nutrimix Laddus (Energy Balls)
*An excellent, sugar-free school snack that satisfies sweet cravings.*

- **Prep Time:** 10 minutes | **Cook Time:** 6–8 minutes | **Yield:** 8–10 small laddus
- **Ideal For:** School-Going Kids, Toddlers, Busy Parents.
- **Ingredients:**
  - 1 cup **Roshini's Nutrimix**
  - 1/2 cup soft, pitted dates
  - 2 tablespoons pure cow's ghee (clarified butter)
  - 1 tablespoon finely chopped dry fruits (cashews, almonds, walnuts)
  - 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
- **Method:**
  1. **Roast:** Dry roast the Nutrimix in a pan on a low flame for 4–5 minutes until it lets out a warm, nutty aroma. Transfer to a bowl and let it cool completely.
  2. **Blend Dates:** Grind the soft, pitted dates in a mixer/blender into a thick, smooth paste.
  3. **Cook Date Paste:** Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee in a pan. Add the date paste and roast it on low flame for 1–2 minutes until it softens and releases oil.
  4. **Mix:** Combine the roasted Nutrimix, warm date paste, remaining ghee, chopped nuts, and cardamom powder in a mixing bowl. Knead gently to blend.
  5. **Shape:** While the mixture is still warm, roll it into small bite-sized balls (laddus). Store in an airtight container. Fresh for up to 3 weeks!

## 2. Classic Chocolate Nutrimix Pudding
*A creamy, chocolatey treat that children love, packed with minerals.*

- **Prep Time:** 5 minutes | **Cook Time:** 8 minutes | **Servings:** 2
- **Ideal For:** Toddlers, teenagers, and chocolate lovers looking for a healthy alternative.
- **Ingredients:**
  - 4 tablespoons **Roshini's Nutrimix**
  - 500ml milk (dairy or almond)
  - 2 tablespoons organic cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  - 3 tablespoons jaggery powder or maple syrup
  - 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
- **Method:**
  1. In a cold saucepan, whisk the Nutrimix, cocoa powder, and milk together until there are no dry pockets or lumps.
  2. Cook on medium-low heat for 6 to 8 minutes, stirring continuously, as the mixture thickens into a glossy pudding.
  3. Stir in the jaggery powder and vanilla extract just before removing from heat.
  4. Pour into serving cups and refrigerate for 2 hours to set. Serve chilled.
