## 1. Feeding & Age Guidelines

### Can I give Nutrimix to my baby?
Yes, absolutely! Millets and dry fruits are traditional baby foods in India. Since our millets are slowly home-roasted, they are highly digestible. You can start introducing a thin Nutrimix malt from **6 to 8 months of age** (once solid foods are started), cooked with water or breast milk/formula. (For a sprouted-only single-grain option, you can also use our Sprouted Ragi Flour).
*Note: Always consult your pediatrician before starting new foods.*

### How is Nutrimix different from commercial baby foods?
Commercial baby food formulations often contain up to 30% added refined sugar, maltodextrin, starch fillers, and artificial chemical flavorings. Roshini’s Nutrimix contains **100% whole grains, nuts, and green cardamom—absolutely nothing else.**

## 2. Ingredients & Allergen Info

### Does this product contain allergens?
Yes. Our standard Nutrimix contains tree nuts: **Almonds, Walnuts, Cashews, and Pistachios**. If anyone in your family has a known nut allergy, please avoid this product. We can prepare nut-free custom batches upon special request.

### Is Nutrimix gluten-free?
Yes. The grains used (Ragi, Bajra, Jowar, Foxtail, and Little millets) are naturally gluten-free. It is excellent for individuals with wheat sensitivity, gluten intolerance, or celiac disease.

## 3. Usage & Health Profiles

### How does Nutrimix help with weight management?
Since millets are rich in complex carbohydrates and dietary fiber, they release energy slowly. A warm bowl of Nutrimix porridge prepared with water or skimmed milk keeps you full for up to 4 hours, preventing unhealthy snacking.

### Can it be used for healthy weight gain in kids?
Yes! To help kids gain healthy weight, cook Nutrimix in full-fat milk, add a teaspoon of pure ghee, and sweeten it with organic jaggery or date paste. The combination of healthy fats from dry fruits and protein from premium grains supports healthy development.

### Is it suitable for diabetics?
Yes, because our standard Nutrimix contains **no added sugar**. The complex carbohydrates and high fiber in Jowar and Bajra have a low glycemic index, preventing rapid blood sugar spikes.

## 4. Production & Quality

### Why do you sprout some of your single-grain flours (like Sprouted Ragi)?
Raw finger millet contains phytic acid which blocks mineral absorption. Soaking and sprouting finger millet for 12 hours activates the enzyme *phytase*, which naturally breaks down phytic acid. This makes the calcium, iron, and zinc easily absorbable by the body, and makes it lighter on baby stomachs.

### How is the product prepared?
Roshini’s Home Products are prepared under strict hygienic protocols. Grains are washed and sun-dried. The standard mixes (like Nutrimix) are slowly home-roasted in small batches to preserve nutrition, while our sprouted products (like Sprouted Ragi) are sprouted for 12 hours before drying. Both are slow stone-ground and packed fresh.
